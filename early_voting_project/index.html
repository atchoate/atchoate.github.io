Early Voting in Texas, 2000-2018

  • While not uniform, early voting as a share of total votes cast is increasing.
  • The most significant spikes occur during presidential election years, and 2008 and 2016 - when no incumbent was on the presidential ballot - saw large increases in early voting.
  • Early voting dropped in non-presidential years, as did overall voter turnout, but early voting's share of the overall total kept increasing.
  • It is reasonable to expect the 2020 election to show a similar amount of both voting and early voting, even though Texas historically lags behind other states in voter turnout. Election officials should prepare for this likelihood, especially if the use of early voting continues to surge among voters.
