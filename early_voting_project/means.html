ABOUT THIS PROJECT

I started looking into this during the 2018 midterm elections. People were flocking to early voting locations and setting turnout records, and I wondered: How many people vote early, and how many wait until Election Day? The answer wasn't as easy to find as I expected it to be, and the numbers revealed something interesting.

The Great Texas Early Voting Surge, 2000-2018

Data can show trends but doesn’t necessarily explain itself. However, there are several possibilities and lines of inquiry that can be explored, including:

  • Increasing partisanship means people have made up their minds and are ready to vote.
  • Organized campaigns want to “lock in” votes once they find support.
  • Early and mail-in voting are more convenient than traveling to a specific polling place on a specific day.
  • Were there changes in early voting policy that helped drive this trend?
  • Did the passage of a Texas voter ID law have an impact?
  • As the Texas population has increased, have there been demographic changes that contribute to increased early voting?

Whatever factors are driving this trend, it is reasonable to expect it to continue when voting starts in the already rancorous 2020 election.
