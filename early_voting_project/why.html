ABOUT THIS PROJECT

I started looking into this during the 2018 midterm elections. People were flocking to early voting locations and setting turnout records, and I wondered: How many people vote early, and how many wait until Election Day? The answer wasn't as easy to find as I expected it to be, and the numbers revealed something interesting.

The Great Texas Early Voting Surge, 2000-2018

It’s not that the state’s statistics are wrong or lying. Those numbers measure early voting turnout against all registered voters, which is a standard way to determine turnout.

Those numbers do not, however, answer the question I had: Of the votes cast in a particular election, how many were cast before Election Day?

It turns out that early and mail-in ballots have made up an increasing share of votes cast in every election since 2000, even in midterm years when Texas’ already dismal voter turnout dropped precipitously.
